Gimme Five!

Posted on Jan 17, 2023.

Set up

In the original gameplay, you are supposed to have 4 players. In our version, there can be anywhere between 2 and many.

The box comes with:

  • a game board
  • 4 pawns of diffferent colors
  • 4 cutouts of cartoon hands matching the pawns
  • 4 tiny boxes for each color filled with the challanges



If you don't have 4 players, but less or more, you will need:

  • the cards
  • your hands
  • pen
  • paper

How to play

Youngest person starts. The game goes in a clockwise rotation

The player on the right picks a random card and asks what point question they want (1 to 3 points). The active player has 15 seconds to list 5 persons/places/synonyms/things or short phrases with the word.
If the time is up, other players raise their hand to attempt an answer. The first hand raised takes the challange.

Whoever answers right gets the amount of points the challange is worth.

For players using the board, use the pawns to move towards the end. Whoever reaches the end first wins.

For players without a board, play until 20 points are earned, or boredom.

Pictionary

Posted on Nov 03, 2023.

Set up

In the original gameplay, you are supposed to have 4 players. In our version, there can be anywhere between 2 and many.

The box comes with:

  • a game board
  • 4 pawns of diffferent colors
  • paper
  • pencils
  • eraser
  • shrapner
  • an hourglass
  • a set of 6 sided dice

If you don't have 4 players, but more, you will need:

  • tiny pawn substitutions
  • more pencil
  • more paper

Each spot on the gameboard has a different color, corresponding with a challange. The colors and rules may vary by gameset, but here's an ideea:

  • Pink: draw with eyes closed
  • Blue: draw using one line
  • Green: draw with non-dominant hand
  • Black: pick at random
  • Red: draw normally

How to play

Each player rolls the dice. The one who rolls highest begins.
They move the pawn to the corresponding spot on the board.
Person on the left of the player picks a card and chooses the word corresponding with the color they landed on. Them and the player have until the hourglass run out to describe the thing on the card and, respectively, draw it.

If the player draws what is on the card correctly, they may advance on the board.

The game runs clockwise.

What Do You Meme?

Posted on Nov 28, 2023.

Set up

  • Meme cards
  • Word cards
  • (optional) Tiny Easel

How to play

One person takes a random meme card from the down-facing deck and (optionally) places it on the easel.
The other players all get 5 word cards and choose the best caption fitting the meme. They place said cards facing down. The person who picked the meme takes them and scrambles them. They then choose which one they like most.The player whose card won gets to keep the meme card.

Play until someone wins 20 cards or boredom.
